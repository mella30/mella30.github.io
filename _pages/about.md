I am a machine learning engineer and deep learning researcher working on reliable perception for real-world environments. My research focuses on robustness, uncertainty estimation, and semantic scene understanding for LiDAR-based perception, with a particular interest in adverse weather, domain shift, and interpretable model behavior.

My background combines machine learning, cognitive science, and applied industrial research. I previously worked on LiDAR perception at Mercedes-Benz AG and on AI and computer vision for robotics at TWT GmbH. I am currently pursuing a Dr.-Ing. in Computer Science at the University of Freiburg on domain robustness in deep learning for LiDAR-based environment perception.

Research
======
My recent work centers on the question of how perception systems can remain reliable when conditions become difficult, sparse, or unfamiliar. This includes:

- uncertainty estimation and calibration for semantic segmentation
- robustness under adverse weather and geospatial domain shifts
- anomaly and novelty detection for safety-critical perception
- structure-aware methods for reliable 3D semantic segmentation

Selected Work
======
**Hierarchical Insights: Exploiting Structural Similarities for Reliable 3D Semantic Segmentation**  
Published at DAGM GCPR 2025. This work studies how hierarchical semantic structure can improve the reliability and interpretability of 3D semantic segmentation. [Paper]({{ base_path }}/publication/hierarchical-insights-exploiting-structural-similarities-for-reliable-3d-semantic-segmentation)

**On the Calibration of Uncertainty Estimation in LiDAR-Based Semantic Segmentation**  
Published at IEEE ITSC 2023. This paper analyzes whether confidence estimates in LiDAR-based semantic segmentation are actually trustworthy and how calibration quality can be evaluated more carefully. [Paper]({{ base_path }}/publication/on-the-calibration-of-uncertainty-estimation-in-lidar-based-semantic-segmentation)

**Survey on LiDAR Perception in Adverse Weather Conditions**  
Published at IEEE IV 2023. This survey reviews how rain, fog, snow, and related conditions affect LiDAR perception and summarizes current strategies for building robust 3D perception systems. [Paper]({{ base_path }}/publication/survey-on-lidar-perception-in-adverse-weather-conditions)
