Supervised a student project (master's thesis) on self-supervised and weakly supervised semantic segmentation for LiDAR-based environment perception.

Main focus areas:

- review of self-supervised, weakly supervised, and unsupervised semantic segmentation methods
- implementation and benchmarking of baseline approaches
- investigation of information priors and similarity objectives for representation learning
- development of ideas for domain generalization, anomaly detection, and novelty detection

The project focused on reducing annotation requirements while improving robustness under domain shift.
